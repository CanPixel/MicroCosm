# **App Name**: MicroCosm

## Core Features:

- BioCell Rendering: Dynamic BioCell: The player's cell features a deformable shape and animated BioComponents that visually react to its movement and consumption, all powered by shaders.
- Ecosystem Design: Stylized visuals: Display visuals with inspiration from electron micrographs and Kurzgesagt, combining scientific realism with colorful diagrams and animations.
- Adaptive Ecosystem Pressure: Deterministic procedural species clusters and escalating threat waves react to the length of a run without requiring a server or machine-learning service.
- Aliveness Indicator: Visual indicators to know which cells (viruses and aggressors) could damage or alter our BioCell, based on aliveness metrics
- Nutrient Gathering: Sugar Consumption: Collect resources for your BioCell through animated interaction to make it grow larger.
- Genome Hijack Counterplay: Giant-virus infections can be resisted through Nucleus RNA interference or a Golgi autophagy purge after the relevant organelle upgrade.
- Infinite World: Stream deterministic ecosystem chunks around the player without invisible world borders.

## Style Guidelines:

- Background color: Deep dark blue (#0B0D1A), reminiscent of deep space to allow bright organisms to stand out.
- Primary color: Vibrant teal (#30D5C8) for cells and interactive elements to give a futuristic yet organic vibe.
- Accent color: Electric purple (#BE29EC) for occasional highlights and special effects. Contrast this vivid pop of colour, analogous to teal, and offset by higher brightness.
- Font: Locally bundled Nunito Sans and Sora keep the HUD readable offline, while ZCOOL KuaiLe gives the MicroCosm title its irregular specimen-label character.
- Minimalist and glowing icons will represent BioComponents, resources, and actions, making them easily identifiable in the dark environment.
- An immersive UI overlay that conveys vital game information such as cell size, energy levels, objectives, and upgrades, following a non-intrusive design approach that respects the visual experience.
- Smooth and organic animations for cell movement, component interactions, and resource collection will enhance the feeling of a living microcosm.
